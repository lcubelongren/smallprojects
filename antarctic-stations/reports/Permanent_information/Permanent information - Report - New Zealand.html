    • Title
      Scope/coverage of the plan
      Antarctica New Zealand Coordinated Incident Management System (CIMS)
      CIMS is used to manage significant incidents (e.g., serious injury); environmental incidents (e.g., fuel spills, biosecurity incursions); technology failures (e.g., power loss at Scott Base, loss of communication between Scott Base and Christchurch), fire response; SAR response; food safety incidents; multiple or mass casualties; natural hazard incidents (e.g., earthquakes); communicable disease outbreaks or pandemics; aircraft incidents; cross-NAP aid responses (e.g., Ant NZ is requested to aid USAP/NSF with an incident); planned events (e.g., TEDx, official visits etc); and incidents for when Ant NZ is tasked by the Rescue Coordination Centre NZ. The attached preplans include Scott Base/Antarctic scenarios only.
      Objective
      CIMS ensures the safety of personnel and increases the efficiency and effectiveness of incident response.

      CIMS Manual Preplans section 2223.pdf
      Title
      Scope/coverage of the plan
      Antarctica New Zealand Fuel Spill Prevention and Response Manual
      Fuel spills as a result of New Zealand's activities in Antarctica.
      Objective
      The purpose of this manual is to: (1) Ensure that personnel have the ability to minimise the health, safety and environmental impacts of any spills which do occur through a rapid and effective response; (2) Raise awareness of the risks of hazardous substances in particular fuels; and (3) Provide awareness of Antarctica New Zealand’s obligation to respond to environmental emergencies under the ‘Liability Arising From Environmental Emergencies’ (Annex VI) of the Protocol on Environmental Protection to the Antarctic Treaty and the requirements of the Health and Safety at the Work (Hazardous Substances) Regulations 2017. Health and Safety at Work (Hazardous Substances) Regulations 2017 (Hereafter referred to as the Regulations).
